SMART CITIES MISSION

The Government of India launched Smart Cities Mission on 25.06.2015. The objective of the Smart Cities Mission is to create world class cities as per the aspirations and needs of the citizens. TUFIDCO is the Mission Directorate for implementation of Smart Cities Mission in Tamil Nadu.

The Government of India have selected 100 cities throughout India to develop as ‘Smart Cities” by upgrading infrastructure and smart applications. It improves the quality of life, efficiency of urban operations and meets the present and future generations with respect to economic, social and environmental aspects. 11 cities have been selected in Tamil Nadu to be developed as ‘Smart Cities’. The selected Smart Cities are Chennai, Coimbatore, Madurai, Thanjavur, Salem, Vellore, Tiruppur, Thoothukudi, Tirunelveli, Tiruchirappalli and Erode.

The implementation of Smart City project is being done by a Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) formed at City-level. Under this Mission, each city will get Rs 500 Cr as Central assistance and Rs. 500 Cr as State grant for implementing various smart cities projects.

Out of 378 projects sanctioned at a cost of Rs.10379.14 Cr, 63 projects at a cost of Rs.248.09 Cr have been completed, 199 projects at a cost of Rs.5502.40 Cr. are under execution and the remaining projects are under various stages of implementation.

INTEGRATED URBAN DEVELOPMENT MISSION (IUDM)

Integrated Urban Development Mission, a State flagship programme, was operational from 2011-12 to 2015-16 (5 years) aiming to improve basic infrastructure facilities like roads, drinking water supply, street lights, sewerage, storm water drains, sanitation, solid waste management, improvement of bus stands and parks in all Corporations (Except Chennai), Municipalities and Town Panchayats. Under this scheme, 13,257 projects were sanctioned at a cost of Rs 9594.83 Crore (fully funded/partly funded). Out of the sanctioned 13,257 projects, 13,214 projects have been completed and the remaining 43 projects are at various stages of implementation.

The scheme was re-launched by Government of Tamil Nadu to be operational from the year 2018-19 onwards with the allocation of Rs 750 Cr per year. 549 projects were sanctioned at a cost of Rs. 1234 Cr during 2018-19 and 2019-20 (till 30th Sep 2019). Out of the total sanctioned projects, 166 no. of projects have been completed and the remaining projects are in various stages of implementation.

JNNURM – URBAN INFRASTRUCTURE AND GOVERNANCE (JNNURM – UIG)

Jawaharlal Nehru National Urban Renewal Mission – Urban Infrastructure and Governance (JnNURM – UIG)

The Government of India have launched the Jawaharlal Nehru National Urban Renewal Mission (JNNURM) during December 2005. The Mission period of JNNURM is 7 years. It was extended for two more years i.e upto March 2014. The Government of India have sanctioned 48 projects at an estimated value of Rs.5250.16 crore (GOI Share: Rs. 2126.76 Cr; GoTN Share: 866.36 Cr; ULB/parastatal agencies share: Rs. 2257.03 Cr). Out of 48 sanctioned projects, 39 projects have been completed, 9 projects are in progress.

Transition Phase projects

The Government of India sanctioned 17 projects for an amount of Rs.867.83 crore. The Government of India share is Rs.371.49 Crore. Out of 17 projects, 3 projects have been completed, 12 projects are in progress and 2 projects have been dropped (Coimbatore 24 X 7 Water supply project and Poonamallee Water Supply).

Urban Infrastructure Development Scheme for Small and Medium Towns (UIDSSMT)

UIDSSMT is one of the components of JnNURM scheme. All the non-mission cities of Tamil Nadu, excluding the mission cities of Chennai, Madurai, Coimbatore and their urban agglomerations, are eligible for financial assistance under this scheme. The mission period of the scheme was 7 years. (2005-06 to 2011-12) and it was extended upto March 2014 for completion of ongoing projects.

122 works at a cost of Rs.882.72 Cr (GOI Share: Rs.706.18 Cr; GoTN Share: Rs.88.27 Cr; ULB Share: Rs.88.27 Cr.) sanctioned under UIDSSMT. Out of the sanctioned 122 projects, 120 projects have been completed and the remaining 2 projects are nearing completion.

UIDSSMT – Transition Phase

The Government of India approved the following 13 projects at a cost of Rs.507.40 Cr under UIDSSMT for the transition phase of JnNURM (2013 – 14). Out of 13 projects, 6 projects have been completed, 6 projects are in progress, 1 project has been dropped (Jolarpet UGD).

METROPOLITAN INFRASTRUCTURE DEVELOPMENT FUND SCHEME (MIDF)

The Mega City Programme (MCP) was launched by Government of India ( GOI ) during 1995 and phased from December 2005. As per the guidelines the sharing between Central and State Government is 25:25 and the balance 50% to be met by the implementing agencies. The funds from Central and State Governments flow directly to the Nodal Agencies(TUFIDCO) as grant. The Nodal agency should give project-based loans at variable rates of interest, with a judicious mix of grants and loans to various implementing institutions. The Nodal agency should create a revolving fund out of repayment made by the implementing institutions for the development of infrastructural assets on continuous basis.

Accordingly, TUFIDCO had created the Revolving fund and the same has been converted as Metropolitan Infrastructure Development Fund ( MIDF ) as approved by GOI. The GOI have stated that the above funds may be utilized for any infrastructure development project on the lines of the guidelines governing the MCP. The total fund so far created under MIDF is Rs 1039.53 crore(as on 31.12.2011).

INFRASTRUCTURE GAP FILLING FUND AND OPERATION & MAINTENANCE GAP FILLING FUND

Based on the Third State Finance Commission’s recommendations, Government will allocate Funds in the Budget for Infrastructure Gap Filling Fund and operation and maintenance of needy infrastructure facilities of urban local bodies. The heads of the departments, O/o Commissionerate of Municipal Administration and Directorate of Town Panchayats will draw the funds from Government and route the same to TUFIDCO.

As per the directions given by the O/o Commissionerate of Municipal Administration and Directorate of Town Panchayats, TUFIDCO will release the fund to the local bodies.

KALAIGNAR NAGARPURA MEMPATTU THITTAM (KNMT)

Government of Tamil Nadu announced the “Kalaignar Nagarpura Mempattu Thittam” (KNMT) scheme to fulfil the Infrastructural Gap in all the 121 Municipalities and 528 Town Panchayats and Nodal Agency is TamilNadu Urban Finance and Infrastructure Development Corporation Ltd (TUFIDCO). Budget Allocation for this scheme is Rs.1,000 Crores in the year 2021-22.

Suggestive list of works to be taken up under this scheme
Water supply :

Source creation/augmentation works, pumping main works, extension of pipeline and construction of OHT/GLR especially in uncovered areas.

Street Lights :

Street Lights will be provided in the needy areas wherever required at regular intervals of at least 30 meters. Preference will be given to relatively backward areas/slums and added / extension areas. Provision for street lights near common facility centers/ community assets will also be given priority.

Roads/Street and Lanes :

Upgradation of Earthen gravel/WBM roads to All weather roads paved with BT,CC and paver blocks.

Community Infrastructure :

New community halls with minimum seating capacity of 100 persons and a minimum area of 200 sq.m will be constructed in the needy Municipalities and Town Panchayats.

Markets :

New Markets will be constructed based on need. Upgradation of markets will also be taken up with adequate infrastructure and modern amenities.

Construction of Modern Libraries / Knowledge Centers :

Modern libraries / Knowledge centers shall be constructed in the Municipalities / Town Panchayats to assist the students and job aspirants to equip themselves for competitive examinations. Facilities like Reading hall, Library computer with internet access and other logistics will be provided.

Other Essential Infrastructure

Construction of modern Bus Stands, improvement of existing Bus stands to modern standards, Rejuvenation of water bodies, procurement of vehicles for Solid Waste Management such as Tiper Lorry, Compactor Vehicle, Battery Operated Vehicle and provision of infrastructure for Solid Waste Management activities, upgradation of existing facilities in crematorium, development of New Parks and Play fields, construction and renovation of Community/Public toilets and any other essential infrastructures will be encouraged to be provided under KNMT. The Director of Municipal Administration and Director of Town Panchayats will send the proposals submitted by the ULBs to the Nodal Agency TUFIDCO to appraise and putup the proposal to the Project Sanctioning and Monitoring Committee for approval and Administrative Sanction.
